Figured Bass

A practice room for thoroughbass. Each exercise is a bass line engraved on the bass staff with figured-bass numerals beneath — either freshly generated or a real Bach chorale with his own figures. Read the figures and realize the harmony at the keyboard — there is intentionally no playback and no answer.

How to read the figures

Numbers are intervals above the written bass note (not Roman numerals); their quality comes from the key. Larger numbers sit on top. Common shorthands:

(none)root-position triad (5/3 understood) 6first-inversion triad (6/3) 6/4second-inversion triad 7root-position seventh chord 6/5 · 4/3 · 4/2inversions of a seventh chord ♯ · ♭ · ♮an accidental alone alters the 3rd above the bass; beside a number it alters that interval 4–3a suspension: the upper voice moves over a held bass

Controls

Source chooses generated lines or real Bach chorales (from the BCFB corpus, with Bach's own figures, transposed for variety). Difficulty (generated only) sets the harmonic vocabulary: 1 = triads and the dominant seventh (blank · 6 · 6/4 · 7); 2 adds ii, leading-tone chords and inverted sevenths (6/5 · 4/3 · 4/2); 3 adds suspensions and secondary dominants; 4 leans further into chromatic harmony; 5 adds mode mixture, the Neapolitan and augmented sixths. Length is the number of two-bar phrases. Key can be fixed or random (keys up to four sharps or flats). Press N for a new line.
